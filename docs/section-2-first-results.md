## Part A: Supercharging the Facebook Lives

Kirsty's fortnightly lives are already brilliant for showing off your products and connecting with your audience. Now we're going to add one simple thing that turns viewers into online buyers: a discount code.

The idea is simple — during each live, Kirsty tells people they can buy what they've just seen on your website, and gives them a special code for money off. After the live, you check how many people actually used it. Over time, you'll see which lives bring in the most sales.

### Step 1: Creating a Discount Code Before Each Live

Do this the day before the live, or on the morning of the live. It only takes a couple of minutes.

- [ ] Open Shopify on your phone or laptop (the app or the website — either works)
- [ ] Find the Sidekick chat (the little chat bubble — you've used this before)
- [ ] Type this to Sidekick: **"Create a 15% discount code called LIVE15 that expires in 48 hours"**
- [ ] Sidekick will create the code for you. Before you move on, check these details:
  - [ ] The discount is **15%** (not a fixed amount)
  - [ ] The code name is exactly **LIVE15** (all capitals, no spaces)
  - [ ] It expires in **48 hours** (so people have until the evening after the live to use it)
- [ ] Write the code down on a bit of paper, or take a screenshot on your phone
- [ ] Text or tell Kirsty the code before the live starts — she needs to know it!

**Top tip:** The 48-hour expiry is important. It gives people a bit of time to go to your website after the live, but it also creates urgency — they can't wait forever. That little bit of pressure helps people actually buy rather than just thinking about it.

---

### Step 2: During the Live (Kirsty's Script Addition)

Kirsty doesn't need to change much about how she does the lives. She just needs to add one thing — mentioning the website and the discount code. Here's what to tell her:

- [ ] Show products as normal — nothing changes here
- [ ] **At least 3 times during the live**, Kirsty should say something like:

> "If you want to buy anything you've seen tonight, head to our website **totallyspellbound.com** and use the code **LIVE15** for 15% off — but be quick, the code expires tomorrow night!"

She can say it in her own words, of course. The key bits are:
  - The website address
  - The discount code
  - The fact that it expires soon

- [ ] **Pin a comment** at the top of the live with this information:

> Shop online at totallyspellbound.com — use code LIVE15 for 15% off! Expires tomorrow night.

To pin a comment: Kirsty (or your daughter-in-law if she's helping) types the comment during the live, then taps and holds on it, and selects "Pin Comment." This keeps it visible at the top so latecomers can see it too.

- [ ] Kirsty should mention the code near the **start**, somewhere in the **middle**, and again right at the **end** of the live. People tune in at different times, so saying it once isn't enough.

---

### Step 3: After the Live (Next Morning Check-In)

This is the exciting bit — finding out if it worked! Do this the morning after the live, or the day after that. Give people a bit of time to place their orders first.

- [ ] Open Shopify and go to Sidekick
- [ ] Ask Sidekick: **"How many orders used discount code LIVE15?"**
- [ ] Write down the number
- [ ] Ask Sidekick: **"What was the total revenue from code LIVE15?"**
- [ ] Write that down too

**Start a simple log.** This can be a notebook, a piece of paper stuck to the wall, or even a note on your phone. Each time, write down:

| Date of Live | Code Used | Number of Orders | Total Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|
| e.g. 15th March | LIVE15 | 4 | £47.50 |
| e.g. 29th March | LIVE16 | 6 | £82.00 |

Over time, this log becomes incredibly useful. You'll start to see patterns — maybe lives where Kirsty shows crystals sell more than lives focused on incense. Maybe Friday night lives do better than Sunday ones. This is real information that helps you plan better.

---

### Step 4: Before the NEXT Live

This bit is really important — don't reuse the same code!

- [ ] For the next live, create a **new** code with a different name
  - Second live: **LIVE16**
  - Third live: **LIVE17**
  - Fourth live: **LIVE18**
  - ...and so on
- [ ] Follow the same steps as before — tell Sidekick to create it, check the details, give the code to Kirsty

**Why a new code each time?** Because if you use the same code every time, you can't tell which live generated which sales. With separate codes, you can look back and say "the live on the 15th brought in £47, but the one on the 29th brought in £82 — what did Kirsty do differently?" That's powerful knowledge.

## Part B: Ordering New Business Cards

Your business cards need updating now that you have a website. Every customer who walks into your shop or visits your craft fair stall should leave with a card that has your web address on it. This is one of the simplest and most effective things you can do.

### Step 1: What the New Cards Should Include

**Front of the card:**
- [ ] Shop name: **Totally Spellbound**
- [ ] Your tagline (if you have one — if not, don't worry, you can always add one later)
- [ ] Website: **totallyspellbound.com**
- [ ] Facebook page link (e.g. facebook.com/totallyspellbound)
- [ ] Instagram handle (e.g. @totallyspellbound) — if you have one

**Back of the card:**
- [ ] Keep your existing loyalty card format — your customers already know and like it, so there's no need to change what works

**Design tip:** Keep it clean and easy to read. The website address should be nice and big — that's the most important new addition. Dark background with light text fits your witchy branding beautifully.

---

### Step 2: Where to Order Them

- [ ] **Vistaprint** (vistaprint.co.uk) is the most popular option — they're affordable, reliable, and often have deals on. Your sons can help you design them using Vistaprint's online tool, or you can upload a design
- [ ] Alternatively, ask a **local printer** — sometimes a local business can do a lovely job, and it keeps things in the community
- [ ] If your sons are handy with design, they could create the card layout using a free tool like Canva (canva.com) and then send it to whichever printer you choose

---

### Step 3: How Many to Order

- [ ] Start with **250 to 500 cards** — this should last you several months
- [ ] That covers giving them out in the shop daily, plus having plenty for craft fairs
- [ ] You can always reorder more — don't feel you need to get thousands straight away

---

### Step 4: Put Them to Work

- [ ] Keep a stack of cards **right by the till** — somewhere you can grab one easily
- [ ] Give one to **every single customer**, even your regulars. Say something like: *"Have you seen our new website? You can order from us online now too!"* — people who love your shop will be excited about this
- [ ] Keep a separate stack in your **craft fair kit** — pack them alongside your card reader and float
- [ ] If you pop a card in with every purchase bag, even better — it's a little reminder for later

## Part C: Your First Data Review (End of Month 2)

This is a really exciting moment, Alison. For the first time in your business, you're going to have real, proper data about what's selling, how much you're making, and where your customers are coming from. No guessing, no gut feelings — actual numbers.

Set aside 15-20 minutes at the end of Month 2 to do this. Make yourself a cup of tea, sit down with your phone or laptop, and have a chat with Sidekick.

### Step 1: Find Out What's Selling Best

- [ ] Open Shopify and go to Sidekick
- [ ] Ask: **"What are my best selling products this month?"**
- [ ] Look at the answer carefully. Ask yourself:
  - Are you surprised by what's at the top?
  - Does it match what you thought sold well?
  - Is there anything you expected to see that isn't there?
- [ ] Write down your top 5 sellers

---

### Step 2: Check Your Online Orders

- [ ] Ask Sidekick: **"How many online orders did I get this month?"**
- [ ] Write down the number
- [ ] Don't worry if it's small — even a handful of online orders in Month 2 is a great start. Remember, most small businesses take 3-6 months to build up steady online sales

---

### Step 3: Check Your Total Revenue

- [ ] Ask Sidekick: **"What was my total revenue this month — online and in-store?"**
- [ ] This number includes everything — till sales recorded through POS and online orders
- [ ] Write it down. This is your baseline. Next month, you'll compare against it

---

### Step 4: Find the Products That Aren't Selling

- [ ] Ask Sidekick: **"Which products haven't sold at all?"**
- [ ] Look at the list. For each product, think about:
  - **Is it priced wrong?** Maybe it's too expensive for what it is, or maybe the price looks odd (£7.49 might sell better as £7 or £7.50)
  - **Is the photo bad?** A dark, blurry photo puts people off. Could you retake it in better light?
  - **Is it just not popular?** Some things don't sell online even if they sell in person. That's OK — at least now you know
- [ ] Pick 2 or 3 non-sellers and try something — better photo, new price, or featuring them in a live

---

### Step 5: Find Out Where Your Customers Are Coming From

- [ ] Ask Sidekick: **"Where are my customers coming from?"**
- [ ] This tells you whether people are finding your shop through:
  - **Social media** (Facebook, Instagram — this means Xyla and the lives are working)
  - **Google search** (people are searching for things like "crystals near me" or "buy crystals online UK")
  - **Direct** (people are typing your website address straight into their browser — this means your business cards, word of mouth, or the lives are working)
- [ ] Write down the main sources

---

### What to Do With This Information

This is where data turns into action. Here's a simple guide:

**If something is selling fast:**
- [ ] Restock it! Order more from your supplier before you run out
- [ ] Consider raising the price slightly if it's flying off the shelves — a £1-2 increase on a popular item can make a real difference over time

**If something isn't moving at all:**
- [ ] Try a lower price and see if that helps
- [ ] Take a better photo — natural daylight, clean background, maybe a nice bit of fabric underneath
- [ ] Ask Kirsty to feature it in a live — sometimes people just need to see it in action

**If social media is your biggest traffic source:**
- [ ] Xyla is doing its job — keep approving those posts!
- [ ] The lives are bringing people to the site — keep doing them

**If most traffic is "direct":**
- [ ] Your business cards and word of mouth are working — keep giving out cards
- [ ] The live viewers are remembering your website address — brilliant

**If Google is sending you traffic:**
- [ ] People are finding you through search — this is free advertising and it tends to grow over time
- [ ] Your product descriptions are helping Google find you — another reason to keep them detailed

## Part D: Adding Personalised Products Online (Month 3)

This is where things get really exciting. Your laser engraver and 3D printer give you something that no big online retailer can offer — truly personalised, handmade items. These are your secret weapon, and it's time to get them onto your website.

### Step 1: Create Your First Personalised Product Listing

- [ ] Open Shopify and go to Sidekick
- [ ] Tell Sidekick: **"Add a new product called 'Personalised Laser Engraved Crystal'"**
- [ ] Sidekick will start creating the product for you

---

### Step 2: Set the Price

- [ ] Set a base price — somewhere around **£15 to £25** depending on the crystal size and type
- [ ] If you want to offer different sizes at different prices, you can tell Sidekick: **"Add variants for small (£15), medium (£20), and large (£25)"**
- [ ] Remember to account for your time, materials, and postage when setting the price

---

### Step 3: Take a Great Photo

- [ ] Find an example piece you've already made — one that shows off the engraving nicely
- [ ] Take a photo in good, natural light (near a window on a bright day is perfect)
- [ ] Use a clean, simple background — a piece of dark velvet or slate works beautifully for crystals
- [ ] Take a couple of photos — one of the whole piece, and one close-up of the engraving detail
- [ ] Upload the photos when Sidekick asks, or add them to the product page afterwards

---

### Step 4: Write a Description

- [ ] Tell Sidekick to write a description. You can say something like: **"Write a product description for a personalised laser engraved crystal"**
- [ ] Or give Sidekick this to work with:

> "Choose your crystal and tell us what you'd like engraved — a name, a date, a symbol, or a short message. Each piece is made to order in our shop and posted within 3-5 working days."

- [ ] Check the description sounds like you — warm, personal, and clear
- [ ] Make sure it mentions that each piece is handmade and unique — that's your selling point!

---

### Step 5: Enable Customer Notes (Very Important!)

This is how customers tell you what they want engraved. Without this, they can't give you their personalisation details.

- [ ] In the Shopify product settings, look for the option that says **"customers can add notes"** or ask Sidekick: **"Enable order notes for customers on my store"**
- [ ] This adds a text box at checkout where customers can type what they'd like engraved
- [ ] You might also want to add a line in your product description that says: **"Please write your engraving request in the notes box at checkout"** — so people know where to put it

---

### Step 6: When an Order Comes In

Here's what your process looks like when someone orders a personalised item:

- [ ] You'll get a notification from Shopify (on your phone or by email) that a new order has come in
- [ ] Open the order and **read the customer's note** — this tells you what they want engraved
- [ ] Make the item in your workshop
- [ ] **Take a photo of the finished piece** — this is a lovely touch. You can send it to the customer before posting to show them what they're getting. People absolutely love this, and it often leads to them sharing it on social media (free advertising for you!)
- [ ] Wrap it carefully and post it
- [ ] Mark the order as fulfilled in Shopify

---

### Step 7: Add More Personalised Products Over Time

Once you've got the hang of listing and fulfilling one personalised product, consider adding more. Here are some ideas:

- [ ] **"Custom 3D Printed Crystal Stand"** — customers choose the colour and size
- [ ] **"Engraved Spell Jar"** — a beautiful jar with a personalised message or spell name
- [ ] **"Personalised Witchy Gift Box"** — a curated box with engraved items, chosen crystals, and maybe some incense

**Remember: these personalised products are your unique selling point.** Amazon and the big online shops simply cannot do what you do. When someone buys a personalised laser-engraved crystal from Totally Spellbound, they're getting something truly one-of-a-kind, made by hand in your little shop. That's special, and people will pay for it.

## Part E: Updating the Craft Fair Schedule

If you've got a craft fairs page on your website (and you should!), it needs to stay up to date. Nothing makes a website look abandoned faster than a list of events that already happened months ago. Keeping it current shows people you're active and gives them a reason to come and find you in person.

### Step 1: When You Book a New Craft Fair

- [ ] Open Shopify and go to Sidekick
- [ ] Tell Sidekick: **"Update my craft fairs page — add [Event Name] at [Location] on [Date], [Time]"**
  - For example: *"Update my craft fairs page — add Midwinter Market at St Mary's Church Hall on Saturday 15th March, 10am-4pm"*
- [ ] Sidekick will update the page for you

---

### Step 2: Check It Looks Right

- [ ] After Sidekick has made the update, go to your website and look at the craft fairs page
- [ ] Check that the event name, location, date, and time are all correct
- [ ] If anything's wrong, just tell Sidekick what needs changing

---

### Step 3: Remove Past Events

- [ ] After a craft fair has happened, tell Sidekick: **"Remove the [Event Name] from my craft fairs page"**
  - For example: *"Remove the Midwinter Market from my craft fairs page"*
- [ ] Do this within a day or two of the event — don't let old events pile up

---

### Step 4: Make It a Habit

- [ ] Every time you book a fair, update the page. Every time a fair passes, remove it
- [ ] A current, well-maintained craft fairs page tells people: *"This shop is active, they're out and about, they care about their business"*
- [ ] A page full of events from three months ago tells people: *"This website might be abandoned"*
- [ ] It only takes a minute each time — just a quick message to Sidekick

## Part G: Key Points to Remember

These are the things that matter most during Months 2-3. If you remember nothing else, remember these:

**Every live needs a UNIQUE discount code** so you can track which lives work best. LIVE15, LIVE16, LIVE17 — a new code each time. This is how you learn what works.

**Give a business card to EVERY customer** — even regulars who've never bought online. You'd be surprised how many people will go home, look at the card, and think "oh, I'll have a browse tonight." Even your loyal in-shop customers might order gifts for friends and family online.

**Don't panic if online sales are slow in Month 2.** Building an online customer base takes time. A few orders a month in the early days is perfectly normal and perfectly fine. It will grow. Be patient with yourself.

**The data from Sidekick is gold.** For the first time in your business, you'll know what actually sells, what doesn't, and where your customers are coming from. This is information that big companies pay thousands for — and you're getting it just by using your Shopify properly.

**Personalised products are your secret weapon.** Nobody else can offer what your laser engraver and 3D printer can do. A personalised crystal from Totally Spellbound is unique, meaningful, and handmade. That's something Amazon will never be able to compete with.

**Keep the craft fair schedule page current.** It takes one minute to update, and it makes your whole website look professional and active. An outdated events page is worse than having no events page at all.

**Don't stop using POS for in-shop sales!** This is really important. The data Sidekick gives you is only useful if every single sale is recorded — online and in-shop. If you start slipping back to paper for some sales, your numbers won't be accurate, and you won't be able to trust what Sidekick tells you. Every sale, every time, through the POS.
