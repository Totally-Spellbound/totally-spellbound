## Part A: The 6-Month Business Review

This is a big moment. You've now got six whole months of real business data sitting in Shopify. That's incredibly valuable — most small businesses don't have this. You do. Let's use it.

### Step 1: Get the Complete Picture from Sidekick

Open Shopify and click on the **Sidekick** chat (the little icon you've been using to ask questions). You're going to ask it a series of questions. Don't worry about asking them all at once — just go through them one by one, and write down the answers. Grab a cup of tea and a notepad for this bit.

**Ask Sidekick each of these questions, one at a time:**

- [ ] "What was my total revenue for the last 6 months — online and in-store combined?"
- [ ] "What was my total online revenue for the last 6 months?"
- [ ] "What are my all-time top 10 best sellers?"
- [ ] "How many total online orders have I had?"
- [ ] "How many repeat customers do I have?"
- [ ] "What's my average order value online?"
- [ ] "Which month was my best month and why?"
- [ ] "Which discount codes generated the most revenue?"

Write each answer down on paper if that's easier for you. Or just keep the chat open and scroll back through it. The important thing is that you now have a real picture of your business — not a guess, not a feeling, but actual numbers.

**Well done. You've just done something most shop owners never bother to do.**

---

### Step 2: Compare to Your Goal

Your original goal was to get online sales to **£100-200 per week**. So let's see where you are.

Look at your total online revenue for the last 6 months. Divide it by 26 (that's roughly how many weeks are in 6 months). That gives you your average weekly online revenue.

**Now, be honest with yourself — where do you land?**

**If you're below £100/week — don't panic.** You're still making money that didn't exist before. Look at the data and ask yourself:
- [ ] Are there products I should be adding? (What do people ask for in the shop that isn't online yet?)
- [ ] Are my Facebook lives driving sales? (If not, try different products or different times)
- [ ] Are craft fairs bringing people to the website? (Are you mentioning the website and handing out cards?)
- [ ] Are there products that get lots of views but nobody buys? (Maybe the price needs adjusting, or the photos need improving)
- [ ] Could I do more with personalised products? (These tend to have higher value)

The data will point you in the right direction. You don't need to guess anymore.

**If you're at £100-200/week — congratulations!** You've hit your target. That's genuinely brilliant. Now think about:
- [ ] What got you there? Was it a particular product? The lives? Craft fairs? Seasonal events?
- [ ] Whatever is working — do more of it
- [ ] Set a new goal. Maybe £200-300/week? Or maybe you're happy here and want to focus on keeping it steady. Both are perfectly fine.

**If you're above £200/week — amazing!** You're ahead of where we hoped you'd be. Time to think about:
- [ ] Could you use some help? (More on this in Part E)
- [ ] Should you expand your product range?
- [ ] Are you able to keep up with orders? If it's getting stressful, that's a sign you need support — and that's a good problem to have!

## Part B: Seasonal Planning

Here's something that's going to make your life so much easier: planning ahead for the calendar. Your niche — witchy, gothic, crystals — has natural peaks throughout the year. If you know they're coming, you can be ready. No more scrambling at the last minute.

### Step 1: Know Your Key Dates

These are the dates that matter most for Totally Spellbound. Some are big, some are smaller, but all of them are opportunities to sell more.

- [ ] **Imbolc** (1st-2nd February) — New beginnings, candles, cleansing. A gentle start to the year.
- [ ] **Valentine's Day** (14th February) — Rose quartz, love stones, heart-shaped items, gift sets for partners.
- [ ] **Spring Equinox / Ostara** (around 20th March) — Growth, renewal, fresh starts. Green and floral themes.
- [ ] **Mother's Day** (date changes each year — check!) — Gift sets, personalised items, treat-yourself boxes.
- [ ] **Beltane** (1st May) — Fertility, flowers, fire energy. Bright and vibrant products.
- [ ] **Litha / Summer Solstice** (around 21st June) — Sun energy, abundance, citrine and sunstone.
- [ ] **Lammas / Lughnasadh** (1st August) — Harvest, gratitude, amber tones, abundance.
- [ ] **Mabon / Autumn Equinox** (around 22nd September) — Balance, reflection, autumn colours.
- [ ] **Samhain / Halloween** (31st October) — **THIS IS YOUR BIGGEST MONTH.** Protection, ancestors, divination, black tourmaline, obsidian, tarot, candles, everything spooky and magical. Go all out.
- [ ] **Yule / Winter Solstice** (around 21st December) — Gifts, warmth, rebirth, pine scents, gold and green.
- [ ] **Christmas** (25th December) — Gift sets, engraved items, mystery boxes, stocking fillers.
- [ ] **Full Moons** (every month) — Each full moon has a different name and energy. Great for themed posts and flash sales.
- [ ] **Friday the 13th** (whenever it falls) — Perfect for themed sales and spooky promotions.

### Step 2: Prepare for Each Key Date (2 Weeks Before)

For every key date on that list, here's what to do about **two weeks beforehand**:

- [ ] **Create a discount code** in Shopify. Keep it simple and themed — for example:
  - "SAMHAIN20" for 20% off during Halloween week
  - "LOVESPELLS" for Valentine's Day
  - "YULE15" for 15% off before Christmas
  - You already know how to do this — you've been creating codes for your lives!

- [ ] **Update your homepage.** Tell Sidekick: "Update the shop homepage to feature our Halloween products" (or whatever the season is). Sidekick can help you rearrange what's front and centre on your website.

- [ ] **Adjust Xyla's posts.** Ask your sons to tweak Xyla so it's posting seasonal content in the run-up. For example, before Samhain, Xyla should be posting about protection crystals, divination tools, and ancestor work — not summer solstice items!

- [ ] **Feature seasonal items in your Facebook live.** When you do your weekly live, make the seasonal products the star of the show. Use the discount code during the live — "Use code SAMHAIN20 this week for 20% off all protection crystals!"

- [ ] **Stock up on relevant items.** Make sure you've got enough of the right products:
  - Protection crystals and black candles before Halloween
  - Rose quartz and heart-shaped items before Valentine's Day
  - Gift sets and stocking fillers before Christmas
  - You know your stock better than anyone — trust your instincts here, backed up by what sold well last year

### Step 3: Create a Simple 12-Month Calendar

This doesn't need to be fancy. A piece of paper on the wall behind your counter works perfectly.

- [ ] Write down all the key dates from the list above for the next 12 months
- [ ] For each date, note three things:
  1. What products to stock or feature
  2. What discount code to create
  3. What to talk about in your Facebook live
- [ ] **At the start of every month**, look at your calendar. What's coming up in the next 4-6 weeks? Start preparing now. This way, nothing catches you off guard.

Being prepared is the difference between scrambling and sailing. And you deserve to sail.

## Part C: Mystery Boxes — A New Product Line

Mystery boxes are absolutely huge in the crystal community. People love them. They sell brilliantly online, they make fantastic gifts, and — here's the best bit — they help you shift stock that's been sitting around for a while. Everyone wins.

### Step 1: Understand What a Mystery Box Is

A mystery box is a curated selection of crystals and witchy items at a fixed price. The customer doesn't know exactly what they'll get — and that's the whole fun of it. You choose the items based on a theme or a value tier. The customer knows they're getting good value, and the surprise element makes it exciting.

Think of it like a lucky dip, but a really beautiful, thoughtful one.

### Step 2: Create 2-3 Mystery Box Listings

Here are three ideas to start with. You can adjust the prices and contents based on what makes sense for your stock:

**Box 1: "Totally Spellbound Mystery Box — Small" (£15)**
- [ ] Include 3-5 small items: tumble stones, a stick of incense or a cone, a small crystal, maybe a mini candle or a charm
- [ ] Aim for the items to be worth around £20-25 at full price — the customer feels like they're getting a bargain

**Box 2: "Totally Spellbound Mystery Box — Large" (£25)**
- [ ] Include 5-8 items, with at least one larger or more special crystal
- [ ] Maybe add a piece of jewellery, a selenite wand, a crystal point, or something with a bit of wow factor
- [ ] Aim for contents worth around £35-40 at full price

**Box 3: "Themed Mystery Box" (£20)**
- [ ] Pick a theme and curate around it. Ideas:
  - "Protection Box" — black tourmaline, obsidian, protective herbs, a black candle
  - "Self-Love Box" — rose quartz, rhodonite, a bath salt sachet, a positive affirmation card
  - "New Moon Box" — moonstone, a journal prompt card, a white candle, cleansing herbs
  - "Beginner Witch Box" — a selection of starter crystals, a guide card, incense, a small pouch
- [ ] You can change the theme each month to keep things fresh

### Step 3: Understand Why These Work So Well

This is worth knowing, because it'll give you confidence that they're worth doing:

- **They clear slow stock.** Those tumble stones that have been sitting in the basket for months? Perfect for mystery boxes. You're not throwing them away — you're giving them a new life in a package that someone is excited to receive.
- **Customers feel they're getting more value than the price.** And they are! That's what makes them come back for more.
- **The surprise element means people post about them on social media.** When someone opens a mystery box, they often film it or photograph it and share it online. That's free advertising for Totally Spellbound.
- **They make brilliant gifts.** People buy them for friends, for birthdays, for "just because." They don't need to know what their friend likes — you've curated it for them.
- **They're easy to pack and post.** One box, one label, done.

### Step 4: Add Them to Shopify

- [ ] Put together an example box for each tier. Make it look beautiful — a nice box or organza bag, some tissue paper, maybe a sprig of dried lavender, and a handwritten note. People absolutely love a personal touch.
- [ ] Take photos of the example boxes. Make them look inviting and magical. Use Photoroom to clean up the backgrounds if needed.
- [ ] Open Sidekick and say: "Write me an enticing product description for a mystery box of crystals and witchy items, priced at £15, containing 3-5 surprise items." Sidekick will write something lovely. Do the same for each box tier.
- [ ] List them on Shopify just like any other product — photo, description, price, stock level.
- [ ] Set the stock to however many you can realistically make right now. You can always increase it later.
- [ ] **Mention them in your Facebook lives!** "We've got mystery boxes on the website — they make amazing gifts and they're flying out!" People love being told something is popular.

## Part D: Expanding the Personalised Range

Your laser engraver and 3D printer are your secret weapons. They're what make Totally Spellbound different from every other crystal shop online. Nobody else in your village — and very few people online — can offer what you can. That's your unfair advantage, and it's time to lean into it even harder.

### Step 1: Review What's Already Working

- [ ] Ask Sidekick: "Which of my personalised products have sold the most?"
- [ ] Ask Sidekick: "What's the average order value for personalised items compared to non-personalised items?"
- [ ] Look at the answers. Personalised items almost always have a higher order value — people expect to pay more for something made just for them.

### Step 2: Think About What Else You Could Offer

Here are some ideas. You don't need to do all of them — just pick one or two that excite you:

- [ ] **Engraved wooden rune sets** — a set of runes with the customer's name or a personal message on the pouch or box
- [ ] **3D printed moon phase displays** — show the moon phase for a specific date (birthday, anniversary, wedding day). These are stunning and very popular.
- [ ] **Personalised altar items** — engraved offering bowls, incense holders with initials, pentacle plaques with names
- [ ] **Custom spell jars with engraved lids** — a spell jar with the person's intention or name engraved on the lid. Beautiful and meaningful.
- [ ] **Name-engraved crystal boxes** — a wooden box with someone's name engraved on it, perfect for storing their crystal collection
- [ ] **3D printed crystal grid templates** — sacred geometry templates that people can use to lay out their crystals. You could even personalise them with a name or intention in the centre.

### Step 3: List Each New Product the Same Way You've Done Before

You know this process now. It's the same every time:

- [ ] Make the item (or a sample of it)
- [ ] Photograph it nicely
- [ ] Use Photoroom to clean up the background
- [ ] Ask Sidekick to write a description
- [ ] List it on Shopify with a price, photo, and description
- [ ] Share it on your Facebook live and let Xyla post about it

You've done this loads of times already. It's second nature now.

### Step 4: Consider "Made to Order" Items

- [ ] For personalised products, you can set a longer dispatch time — say 5-7 working days instead of 1-2. This means you don't need to hold stock of every variation. You make it when someone orders it.
- [ ] In your Shopify listing, make it clear: "This item is made to order and will be dispatched within 5-7 working days." Customers are absolutely fine with this for personalised items — they expect it to take a bit longer because it's being made just for them.
- [ ] This takes the pressure off. No need to have a shelf full of pre-made items. Just make them as the orders come in.

### Step 5: Price These Items Properly

This is important, Alison. **Do not undersell yourself.**

- [ ] Think about what goes into each item: the materials, the time it takes you to make it, the electricity for the machines, the packaging, the postage
- [ ] Your skill and your equipment are valuable. Not everyone has a laser engraver. Not everyone can do what you do. That's worth something.
- [ ] A good rule of thumb: materials cost + at least £10-15 for your time + postage + a bit of profit on top
- [ ] Look at what similar items sell for on Etsy or other online shops. You'll probably find you can charge more than you think.
- [ ] **You are not "just a little shop in a village." You are a skilled maker with professional equipment. Price like it.**

## Part E: Considering Hiring Help

You've mentioned wanting to employ someone eventually. That's a lovely goal, and it might be closer than you think. Let's work out whether you're ready.

### Step 1: Signs You Need Help

Read through this list and see how many ring true for you:

- [ ] You're getting more online orders than you can comfortably pack and post during your lunch break
- [ ] You're sometimes missing orders or posting them late because you're too busy serving customers in the shop
- [ ] You can't keep up with restocking — items sell out and it takes you days to get them back online
- [ ] You want a day off (or even an afternoon!) but you can't close the shop
- [ ] You're turning down craft fair opportunities because you can't leave the shop unattended
- [ ] You're feeling stretched thin and it's starting to feel more stressful than fun

If you ticked three or more of those, it's probably time to think about getting some help.

### Step 2: Your Options

You don't need to hire a full-time employee. There are simpler options:

**Option 1: A part-time shop assistant (a few hours a week)**
- [ ] Someone who can cover the shop counter while you're packing orders in the back, doing a craft fair, or having a well-earned afternoon off
- [ ] This could be just one or two days a week, or even just Saturday mornings
- [ ] They don't need to know everything about crystals — you can train them. They just need to be friendly and reliable.

**Option 2: A packing helper**
- [ ] Someone who comes in for a few hours a week specifically to pack and post online orders
- [ ] You prepare the orders, they pack them up, print the labels, and take them to the post office
- [ ] This frees you up to focus on the shop, making custom items, and doing what you love

**Option 3: Family help**
- [ ] Could your daughter, daughter-in-law, or someone else in the family take on some tasks?
- [ ] Maybe someone could handle the packing, or cover the shop for a morning, or help at craft fairs
- [ ] Even a few hours a week would make a difference

### Step 3: When the Numbers Support It

Let's look at this practically:

- [ ] If your online revenue is consistently **£150-200+ per week**, that's roughly **£600-800 per month** in online sales alone
- [ ] A part-time helper for a few hours a week might cost around **£200-300 per month** (depending on hours and the current minimum wage)
- [ ] Ask yourself: if hiring someone freed you up to make more personalised products, do more craft fairs, or do more Facebook lives — would you earn more than the £200-300 you're paying them?
- [ ] In most cases, the answer is yes. Hiring help isn't a cost — it's an investment that pays for itself by freeing up your time to do the things that make the most money.

There's no rush on this. But when the time feels right, you'll know. And the numbers will back you up.

## Part J: What's Next? (Beyond 6 Months)

Once you've hit your stride and everything feels comfortable, there are more avenues you could explore. None of these are urgent. They're just ideas for when — and if — you're ready.

- **eBay:** Shopify can sync with eBay if you ever want to revisit it. The fees and rules may have changed since you last looked, and having Shopify manage the listings makes it much less painful than before.

- **TikTok Shop:** If your TikTok following grows (and with Xyla posting daily, it might!), you can sell directly through TikTok. Your sons can help you set this up when the time comes.

- **Wholesale:** Other witchy shops might want to stock your personalised items. If you're making engraved rune sets or 3D printed moon phase displays, smaller shops without that equipment might love to buy them from you in bulk at a trade price.

- **Workshops:** Offer crystal workshops or laser engraving experiences in your shop. "Make Your Own Crystal Grid" or "Engrave Your Own Rune Set" — advertise them online, charge per person, and you've got a whole new income stream plus people visiting your shop.

- **Subscription Box:** A monthly mystery box subscription. Customers sign up and receive a curated box every month. That's recurring revenue — money you can rely on every single month. Shopify has tools to help set this up when you're ready.

- **Pop-up Shops:** If you become known online, other towns and markets might invite you for pop-up events. More visibility, more customers, more fun.

These are just ideas. They'll be here whenever you want them. No rush at all.
