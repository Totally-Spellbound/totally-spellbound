### Step 2: Choose and Set Up a Theme

The "theme" is just what the shop looks like online — the colours, the layout, the feel. You want something that suits Alison's witchy, gothic, crystal-loving brand.

### Step 4: Configure Shipping Rates

1. In **Settings**, click **Shipping and delivery**.
2. Under **Shipping**, you should see a section called **General shipping rates** (or similar). Click **Manage**.
3. Delete any default rates that are already there.
4. Now add three shipping rates:

**Rate 1: Small and light items**
- Click **Add rate**.
- Name it: **Standard Shipping (Small Items)**
- Set the price to: **£2.50**
- Click on **Add conditions** and choose **Based on item weight**.
- Set the weight range from **0g** to **500g**.
- Click **Done** or **Save**.

**Rate 2: Larger and heavier items**
- Click **Add rate** again.
- Name it: **Standard Shipping (Larger Items)**
- Set the price to: **£4.50**
- Click on **Add conditions** and choose **Based on item weight**.
- Set the weight range from **500g** upwards (500g to, say, 30,000g or whatever the maximum is).
- Click **Done** or **Save**.

**Rate 3: Free delivery on orders over £30**
- Click **Add rate** again.
- Name it: **Free Delivery**
- Set the price to: **£0.00**
- Click on **Add conditions** and choose **Based on order price**.
- Set the minimum to **£30** and leave the maximum blank (or set it very high).
- Click **Done** or **Save**.

5. Double-check all three rates are showing correctly.
6. Make sure the shipping zone is set to **United Kingdom** (you can add international later if Alison ever wants to — keep it simple for now).

## Part B: Adding Your Products (One or Two Sundays)

Right, Alison — this is your part. This is where you add all your lovely products to the online shop. Set aside a Sunday (or two) for this. Put the radio on, make a brew, and work through them one at a time. It's actually quite satisfying once you get going.

You're going to do the same steps for every single product. After the first three or four, you'll be doing it on autopilot.

### Before You Start: Set Up Your Photo Station

You don't need anything fancy. Here's what to do:

1. Find a table near a window in your shop — you want natural daylight coming in from the side, not from behind you.
2. Take a piece of **white card** (A3 or bigger is ideal — even a white pillowcase laid flat works in a pinch) and lay it flat on the table. This is your background.
3. If you can, prop another piece of white card up vertically at the back — this gives you a nice clean backdrop.
4. **No flash.** Turn the flash off on your phone camera. Natural light is your best friend, especially with crystals.
5. Bring your **kitchen scale** into the shop and set it up near your photo station. You'll need it to weigh each item.

That's it. That's your "photography studio."

---

### Tips for Photographing Crystals

Crystals are gorgeous but they can be tricky to photograph because they're shiny, see-through, or sparkly. Here are some tips:

- **Always use natural daylight.** Never use flash — flash bounces off crystals and makes them look washed out, flat, or blurry. Daylight brings out the colour and depth.
- **Side lighting is best.** Have the window to one side of the item, not directly behind or in front of it. This gives the crystal some depth and shadow.
- **Get close, but keep the whole item in frame.** Don't chop off the edges. Leave a little space around it.
- **Take 2 or 3 shots of each item** and pick the best one. Slightly different angles can make a big difference.
- **For translucent stones** (like clear quartz, citrine, or amethyst points), try placing them so the light shines through from behind slightly — this shows off the beautiful colour.
- **For dark stones** (like obsidian, black tourmaline), make sure there's enough light that you can see the shape and texture — they can just look like dark blobs if the light isn't good enough.
- **Wipe fingerprints off first!** A quick polish with a soft cloth makes a noticeable difference.

### The Step-by-Step Process for Each Product

Do this for every single item. It gets quicker each time, promise.

---

**Step 1: Pick up the item and weigh it**

- [ ] Place the item on your kitchen scale.
- [ ] Write down the weight in grams on a piece of paper (or a sticky note). For example: "Rose quartz tumble stone — 45g."
- [ ] If you have several of the same item (like you've got ten tumble stones that are all basically the same), just weigh one. They'll all be roughly the same weight.

---

**Step 2: Place it on the white card and take a photo**

- [ ] Put the item in the middle of your white card, near the window.
- [ ] Open your phone's camera.
- [ ] **Make sure the flash is off.** (Look for a little lightning bolt icon on your camera screen — tap it until it says "off" or shows a line through it.)
- [ ] Hold your phone steady, about 20-30cm above or in front of the item.
- [ ] Make sure the whole item is in the picture with a bit of space around the edges.
- [ ] Take **2 or 3 photos** from slightly different angles.
- [ ] Have a quick look at them and pick the best one — the clearest, best colour, nicely centred.

---

**Step 3: Clean up the photo in Photoroom**

- [ ] Open the **Photoroom** app on your phone.
- [ ] Tap the big **"+"** button (or **"Start from photo"** or **"Create"** — it might vary slightly depending on the version).
- [ ] Choose the photo you just took from your camera roll.
- [ ] Photoroom will automatically detect your product and remove the background. You'll see the crystal on a clean white background like magic.
- [ ] If the cutout looks good (it usually does), you can leave it as is.
- [ ] If you want to adjust the background colour, there's usually an option to tap the background and change it — white is perfect for now.
- [ ] Tap **Save** or **Export** or the **download arrow** to save the clean image to your phone's camera roll.

That's it — you now have a lovely, professional-looking product photo.

---

**Step 4: Add the product in Shopify using Sidekick**

- [ ] Open the **Shopify** app on your phone.
- [ ] Tap the **Sidekick** icon (the little sparkle/star icon at the top).
- [ ] Type a message like this (use your own words, it doesn't have to be exact):

   > **"Add a new product called Amethyst Tumble Stone, price £3.50, weight 45g, I have 8 in stock"**

- [ ] Sidekick will create the product for you. It might ask you to confirm the details — just check them and say yes.

---

**Step 5: Add the photo**

- [ ] Once the product is created, open it (Sidekick should take you there, or you can find it under **Products** in the app).
- [ ] Look for where it says **"Add image"** or **"Add media"** — there'll be a camera or image icon.
- [ ] Tap it, and choose the cleaned-up photo from your camera roll (the one you saved from Photoroom — it'll be the most recent photo).
- [ ] The photo uploads and attaches to the product. Done.

---

**Step 6: Get Sidekick to write a description**

- [ ] Go back to Sidekick and type something like:

   > **"Write a description for this amethyst tumble stone, it's a beautiful deep purple stone, good for calm and clarity, about 2cm across"**

   Just describe it in your own words — like you'd describe it to a customer in the shop. Sidekick will turn your words into a proper product description.

- [ ] Read what Sidekick writes back. Does it sound right? Does it match the item?
   - If it's too long, tell Sidekick: **"Make it shorter"**
   - If it's missing something, tell Sidekick: **"Mention that it's good for meditation"** or **"Add that it comes gift-wrapped"**
   - If it doesn't sound like you, tell Sidekick: **"Make it warmer and friendlier"**
- [ ] When you're happy with it, Sidekick will save the description to the product.

---

**Step 7: Double-check and save**

- [ ] Open the product one more time and have a quick look:
   - Is the name right?
   - Is the price right?
   - Is the weight right?
   - Does the photo look good?
   - Does the description sound right?
   - Is the stock count correct?
- [ ] If everything looks good — you're done with this one! Move on to the next item.

---

**Now repeat Steps 1-7 for every product.**

Start with your best sellers — the items people always pick up and buy. You can add more over time. Aim for **at least 10 products** in your first session. If you get on a roll and do 20 or 30, even better!

**A rough guide for how long this takes:** The first product might take you 10-15 minutes as you figure things out. By the fifth product, you'll be doing each one in about 3-5 minutes. So 20 products might take you a couple of hours — put a good playlist on and enjoy it.

## Part C: Your New Daily Routine

This is the part where it all comes together. Once everything is set up and your products are in the system, you just need a few simple habits. None of these take long — we're talking minutes, not hours.

---

### 1. When Someone Buys Something in the Shop

Instead of just putting the money in the till, you're going to ring it up on your phone first. This keeps everything tracked — sales, stock levels, the lot.

- [ ] Pick up your phone and open the **Shopify POS** app.
- [ ] You'll see your products listed on the screen (with pictures, if you've added them). Find the item the customer is buying and **tap on it**.
- [ ] If they're buying more than one item, tap each one. They'll add up in the cart on screen.
- [ ] Tap **Checkout** (usually a big button at the bottom).
- [ ] Choose how they're paying:
   - **Card:** If you have a Shopify card reader, the customer taps or inserts their card. Follow the prompts on screen.
   - **Cash:** Tap **Cash**, then type in the amount the customer is handing you (for example, £10). The app will tell you how much change to give back (for example, "Change due: £3.50").
- [ ] The sale is done. The app records it. The stock count goes down by one automatically.

That's it. Tap, tap, checkout, done.

---

### 2. When Someone Asks for a Custom or Personalised Item

You do custom engraving and 3D printing — so sometimes a customer asks for something that isn't a standard product in your shop. Here's how to record those sales:

- [ ] Do the custom job as you normally would — nothing changes there.
- [ ] When they come to pay, open **Shopify POS** on your phone.
- [ ] Instead of tapping a product from the list, tap **Custom Sale** (you'll find this option on the main screen, usually labelled "Custom sale" or with a "+" icon).
- [ ] Type in a short description of what you made. For example:
   - "Custom engraved rose quartz heart — Emma"
   - "3D printed dragon ornament — blue"
   - "Personalised incense holder — Happy Birthday Jane"
- [ ] Enter the price you're charging.
- [ ] Process the payment as cash or card, just like a normal sale.

This means all your custom work is tracked too — you'll be able to see exactly how much you're earning from personalised items.

---

### 3. When an Online Order Comes In

This is the exciting bit — someone on the internet has found your shop and bought something! Here's what to do:

- [ ] You'll get a **notification on your phone** (a little ping or buzz) from the Shopify app telling you there's a new order. You might also get an email.
- [ ] Open the **Shopify** app on your phone.
- [ ] Tap on **Orders** — you'll see the new order at the top.
- [ ] Tap on it to see the details:
   - What they ordered
   - Their name and delivery address
   - How much they paid (including shipping)
- [ ] Find the item in your shop stock.
- [ ] Wrap it up carefully:
   - **Small, light items** (tumble stones, small crystals, incense sticks): Wrap in tissue paper, then bubble wrap, and pop in a **padded envelope**.
   - **Larger or fragile items** (clusters, points, spears, ornaments): Wrap in bubble wrap, place in a **small box** with some scrunched-up paper or packing material around it so it doesn't rattle about.
   - **A nice touch:** Pop in a little thank-you card or a business card. Customers love that personal feel — it's what makes buying from a small shop special.
- [ ] Write the customer's address on the package clearly, or print a label if you have a printer.
   - Don't forget to put your return address on the back too.
- [ ] **Take it to the post office.** You can drop it off on your way home or first thing the next morning. For items under 500g, Royal Mail second class is fine and affordable. For heavier items, check the pricing at the counter.
- [ ] Once you've posted it, go back into the Shopify app:
   - Open the order.
   - Tap **Mark as shipped** (or **Mark as fulfilled**).
   - If you have a tracking number from the post office, you can pop it in — but this is optional for standard Royal Mail. It's nice to include if you have it, though.
   - The customer will get an automatic email telling them their order is on its way.

**Aim to post items within 1-2 working days of the order coming in.** Customers expect quick dispatch from small shops — it's one of the things that earns you great reviews.

---

### 4. Checking Xyla AI (2-3 Times a Week)

Xyla is working away in the background, creating social media posts about your products. But it doesn't post them until you say so — you're always in control.

- [ ] Open the **Xyla** app (you'll find it in the Shopify apps section, or it might have its own app on your phone).
- [ ] Look at the **queued posts** or **upcoming posts** section.
- [ ] For each post, you'll see:
   - The **image** it's chosen (usually one of your product photos)
   - The **caption** it's written (a short description and some hashtags)
   - Which **platform** it's going to (Facebook, Instagram, TikTok)
   - When it's **scheduled** to post
- [ ] Read through each one:
   - **If it looks good:** Tap **Approve** (or the tick/checkmark button). That post will go out at the scheduled time.
   - **If the caption needs tweaking:** Tap **Edit** and change the words to something you prefer. Maybe it's got a detail wrong, or you want to add "perfect gift idea" or "only 2 left!" Then approve it.
   - **If the image isn't right:** You can swap it for a different product photo. Then approve it.
   - **If you really don't like it:** Skip it or delete it. Xyla will generate more.

**You don't need to spend long on this.** Five or ten minutes, two or three times a week, is plenty. The goal is just to make sure nothing odd goes out under your shop's name. Xyla does the heavy lifting — you're just the quality checker.

## Part E: Key Points to Remember

These are the things worth keeping in the back of your mind as you settle into the routine. You don't need to memorise them — just come back and re-read this bit if you're ever unsure.

- **Always review Sidekick's descriptions before saving.** Sidekick is clever, but it doesn't know your products the way you do. It might say "small purple stone" when you know it's actually a "gorgeous deep violet amethyst point with beautiful natural banding." Give it a read, tweak if needed, then save.

- **Take product photos in natural daylight, never with flash.** This is the single biggest tip for making your products look good online. Crystals look terrible with flash — they go flat and washed out. Natural side-lighting makes them glow.

- **If Photoroom's free version adds a watermark to your photos,** don't panic. Ask your sons about upgrading to the paid version (it's not expensive) or finding an alternative app. A watermark on your product photos doesn't look professional, so it's worth sorting out.

- **When something sells in the shop AND is listed online, POS updates the stock automatically** — as long as you ring the sale through on POS. This is why it's important to use POS for every sale, even cash ones.

- **If you sell something in the shop and forget to use POS** (it happens, don't worry), just open Sidekick later and type: **"Reduce stock of [item name] by 1."** This keeps your online stock count accurate so you don't accidentally sell something online that you've already sold in the shop.

- **Check for online orders at least once a day.** Customers buying online expect their items to be dispatched quickly. A quick glance at the Orders section in the app takes ten seconds.

- **Post items within 1-2 working days of the order.** This is the standard that customers expect from small online shops. If there's a delay (say you're at a craft fair all weekend), just drop the customer a quick message through Shopify to let them know — they'll appreciate the communication.
