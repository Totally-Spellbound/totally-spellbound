## Who Is Alison?

- **Name:** Alison
- **Age:** 65
- **Shop:** Totally Spellbound — a witchy/gothic crystals and gifts shop
- **Location:** Small village highstreet, England
- **Tech comfort:** Not very confident with technology but willing to learn. Comfortable using her phone for photos and messages. Has a laptop too.
- **Key trait:** She's capable but her daughter Kirsty tends to take over ("don't worry, I'll do it"), which stops Alison building confidence. The goal is for Alison to do things herself with AI tools helping her.
- **Communication:** Alison speaks plainly and directly. She appreciates warm, patient explanations with no jargon. Use pounds (£) not dollars.

## The Family

- **Sons:** Paying for and helping set up Shopify. Handle tech setup. Available for one-time configuration tasks.
- **Daughter Kirsty:** Runs the Facebook lives fortnightly. Posts on the shop's Facebook page and occasionally TikTok. She's the "face" of the lives.
- **Daughter-in-law:** Backup for Facebook lives when Kirsty isn't available.

## The Business

- **Products:** Crystals (tumble stones, points, spears, amethyst, rose quartz etc.), incense, witchy/gothic gifts
- **Price range:** £1 to £65
- **Unique capabilities:** Alison owns a laser engraver and a 3D printer — she makes personalised/custom products (engraved crystals, custom items). This is her competitive advantage.
- **Stock:** Under 50 products initially, wholesale sourced from suppliers + custom made items
- **Weekly shop takings:** £100-250
- **Craft fairs:** Currently 2/month, wants to grow to 4/month. Makes £100-250 per fair (carnivals are the best)
- **Online sales before this plan:** £0 — no online shop
- **Previous online experience:** eBay worked well until fees made it unviable. Had a website for over a year, got 1 sale (traffic problem, not content problem).

## Current Online Presence

- **Facebook page:** Exists, daughter Kirsty manages it
- **Facebook lives:** Fortnightly, Kirsty presents, Alison is in the background. Viewership dwindled from decent numbers to 1-8 viewers.
- **TikTok:** Occasional posts by Kirsty
- **Instagram:** Being set up as part of this plan
- **Website:** Previously had one that failed (no traffic). Now setting up Shopify.

## Previous Pain Points (Before This Plan)

- **No digital sales records** — everything written on paper
- **No stock take** — last one was years ago, relies on memory
- **No business data** — can't tell what sells well vs what doesn't
- **Technology is daunting** — looked at Shopify before but the process felt overwhelming
- **Stock sync concern** — if she sells something online that's also in the shop, how does she manage that?
- **Facebook lives dwindling** — algorithm throttling reach, low viewership
- **Doesn't want to do lives herself** — self-conscious about her voice

## The Agreed Plan: Approach B — "Confident Launch"

### Tools Chosen

| Tool | Purpose | Status |
|------|---------|--------|
| **Shopify Basic** (£25/mo, sons pay, £1/mo first 3 months) | Online store + POS for in-shop sales | To be set up |
| **Shopify Sidekick** (free, built in) | Manage store via plain English chat — descriptions, discount codes, data queries, page updates | To be set up |
| **Shopify POS Lite** (free, built in) | Phone as till for in-shop sales — cash and card, tracks stock | To be set up |
| **Xyla AI Growth** (£29/mo, free tier first) | Auto-generate and schedule social media posts from products | To be set up |
| **Photoroom** (free tier has watermark — may need paid ~£9/mo) | Clean up product photos on phone | To be set up |

### Tools NOT Using (and why)

- **Etsy:** Fees too high, poor customer service — Alison had bad experience
- **Predis AI:** Auto-posting starts at £40-79/mo (too expensive), confusing credit system, billing complaints
- **SocialPilot:** 2.5 star rating, 73% one-star Trustpilot reviews, poor support
- **AutoPost by Amasty:** Only 12 reviews — too new to trust
- **Outfy:** Runner-up but billing complaints and hidden "Smart Q" setting issue

### Shipping Setup

- **Flat rate to start:** £2.50 small items / £4.50 larger items / free over £30
- **Product weights recorded** from day one so she can switch to calculated Royal Mail rates later
- **Royal Mail calculated rates** require a third-party Shopify app (not built in) — sons to install when ready
- **Alison posts orders herself** — wrap, label, drop at post office, mark as shipped in Shopify

### Key Decisions Made

- All products added in one or two Sunday sessions (not gradually)
- Xyla AI set up from day one (no waiting)
- POS used for ALL in-shop sales from day one (replacing paper)
- Custom/personalised orders: "Custom Sale" on POS for in-shop, proper product listings for online
- Cash sales: fully supported in Shopify POS
- Each Facebook live gets a unique discount code to track performance
- Business cards updated with website URL + Facebook + loyalty card
- Craft fair schedule page on the website
- GDPR: email marketing requires customer opt-in (can't pre-tick the box in UK)
- Personalised products listed online (customer adds engraving request in order notes)
